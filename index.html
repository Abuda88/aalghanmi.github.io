About Me

My name is Abdulrahman Alghanmi, a dedicated Saudi public servant with a deep commitment to public service, diplomacy, and international engagement. My professional journey began in 2010, immediately after high school, when I joined the Ministry of Health as a Human Resources Officer. In 2014, I was awarded a government scholarship and moved to the United States to further my education and contribute to global advocacy.

I earned a Bachelor‚Äôs degree in Political Science and an Associate of Arts in Humanities and Social Sciences from Utah Valley University. During my time at UVU, I was actively involved in student-led diplomacy and international cooperation. This included participating in the 63rd and 65th Sessions of the UN Commission on the Status of Women (CSW63 and CSW65), the 68th UN Civil Society Conference, and the Mountain Partnership advocacy efforts. I also served as Vice President of both the Sustainable Mountain Development Club and the Foreign Affairs Club.

My dedication to service extends back home, with over 1,000 volunteer hours recorded through the Saudi National Center for Non-Profit Sector. I currently work at the Internal Audit Department at the Jeddah Second Health Cluster.
